Wearing and Putting on Clothes

Download this lesson's word list as an Anki deck!

Naason holikfahchi? What are you wearing?

Wilo

shoe

Wearing shirts, pants, shoes, and hats

Unlike in English where we have one word to wear every piece of clothing, in Alabama, you must use a different verb depending on what you are wearing.

Putting on versus wearing: Doubled-consonant form

In Alabama, the same basic verb can be used, but in different forms, to mean that someone 'put on' a piece of clothes, and that someone is 'wearing' it. To wear a piece of clothing, we use the doubled-consonant form of the verb. To say that someone is just putting it on, we use the single-consonant form of the verb.

In this verb form, the second to last consonant of the dictionary form is doubled.

Yaatala yaatalaaliti

She/he put on a hat.

Yaatala yaatallaaliti

She/he was wearing a hat.

If the dictionary form of the verb is only two syllables long, and has a doubled vowel in the first syllable, there is no difference in the 'putting on' and 'wearing' forms!

Wilon haaɬiti

She/he put on shoes.

Wilon haaɬiti

She/he was wearing shoes.

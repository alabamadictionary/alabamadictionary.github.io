Basic Noun Grammar

This page details the basic systems underlying noun grammar. Nouns in Alabama are fairly simple: They can take four different cases which tell us the role the noun has in the sentence. In Alabama the four cases are:

  • Nominative (nom.), used for marking subjects of verbs
  • Oblique (obl.), used for marking direct objects, indirect objects, and other elements of discourse, including prepositional objects.
  • Topic (top.), used for marking the topic of a phrase. This also can be used for copular expressions.
  • Locative (loc.), used exclusively to mark where something is.
Together, these cases form the paradigm:
CaseMarker
nom.-k
obl.-n
loc.-fa
top.-ya

Locative Case

This case is irregular, in that it may only surface as -fa before the topic marker. Everywhere else, it is realized as -fon

Dallaskafon aɬiinalo.

I'm going to Dallas.

-fa can mean either 'to', 'from', 'in', or 'on', depending on context!

ischobafon aɬɬayaɬicholati yahmifookama.

She always goes to church at this time.

The "Plural" marker -ha

Most nouns can be either singular or plural when they are by themself. But some nouns, especially those referring to people, can take a special plural suffix -ha

tayyiha wiihayok Ava holban hoimalostitolo.

All the ladies likes Ava's drawing.
